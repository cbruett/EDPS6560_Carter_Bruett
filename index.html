My teaching philosophy

Accessibility

Students deserve an educational experience that is accessible. Barriers to learning should be minimized in order for maximum impact during learning hours. This includes providing content that acknowledges and respects the cultural differences we each bring to the classroom. Content is always made available in multiple formats to accommodate learning across multiple platforms. Mental health and general wellness is always in focus. Adult cognitive learning theories are incorporated in every activity.


Clinicians depend heavily on empathy in order to form deep connections with patients. Providing healthcare inherently requires trust between the provider and the patient. Empathy is an absolute keystone in the development of trust. All curricular processes are designed to help students build empathy for those around them. Social determinants of health are considered in all coursework.

Empathy is also important within a school. Students are the focus in every educational process and are treated with empathy to foster strong relationships.

Empathy


Modularity

Course curricula are built from modular components which allow flexibility and accommodation. Courses naturally build on themselves without requiring rigid structure. Students should be able to easily weave in and out of the curriculum and individually extract the learning objectives using methods they are comfortable with.
